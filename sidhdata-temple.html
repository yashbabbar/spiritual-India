Sidhdata Temple

Jagadguru Sudarshanacharya ( born : 27 May 1937, died : 22 May 2007) was an eminent Hindu saint of the Vaishnava sect founded by Sri Ramanujacharya who had innumerable provenances from Tapobal. Born in a Brahmin caste farmer family in the village of Padla in Sawai Madhopur district of Rajasthan , this boy was disowned with religion at the age of only three and a half years. Due to arousing religious interest from childhood, he adopted the best religion of human service at the age of seven. After serious study of Vedas , Puranas and Philosophies , he did penance continuously for twelve years and got the psychic power to know the past, future and present through meditation yoga. He established the Siddhatda Ashram in 1990 on Badhkal Surajkund Road near Faridabad , which nowadays has taken the form of a famous Siddhpeet.

In 1998, all saints Mahatma gathered in the Mahakumbh of Haridwar and all unanimously conferred upon him the title of Jagadguru . The dhuna established by him in the ashram remains smoldering even today under the influence of his austerity, as he used to smoke while he was alive. Sudarshanacharya has now become Brahmalin (late), but his Charanpadukas (standing) have been established near his samadhi for the devotees.

Even today, his devotees hold a fair in the Siddhada Ashram. Every person who comes here, irrespective of religion, caste or creed , is allowed to enter without any discrimination. Presently, Swami Purushottam Acharya hears the problem of devotees visiting here and according to Siddhapeeth's knowledge, suggests its solution. This ashram has now become a pilgrimage center for millions of people coming from abroad.
